Buildathon

First of its kind virtual hackathon for Women and Allies.

Project Brief

I was responsible for designing the very first virtual Hackathon focusing on Women's Rights. I originally took over for the previous designer when it was still in the ideating and creating low fidelity wireframes for the landing page. Since this was a unique event, I had to be creative and innovating while working with a cross-functional team of designers and engineers. By the end of the Buildathon, we had 235 Participants.

Project Goals Key Challenges Target Users
Create a virtual event for builders, mentors, and sponsors. Meeting deadlines during a short amount of time and maintaining communication. Women and allies entering the tech field.

Challenges

6 weeks from onboarding to Kickoff. “How might we help our participants see the workshops they are interested in?” Communication with a remote team.

Solution

Dedicated to work full-time to the project. I researched other tech events with workshops and took some ideas and improved on others. Weekly Google Meet for feedback and updates

Inspiration

We wanted a “futuristic” and “empowering woman” feel. So we took inspiration from other websites to get an idea where we want to go forward.

Wireframes

Figure A - Lo-fi Mobile Wireframes and filter

Following a mobile-first approach, I needed to figure out how to display events and place a filter. Originally, I was going to place specific dates on the filter, but then I realized it looks very cluttered and takes up too much space.


Figure B - Hi-fi Events page

First version of events page for desktop and mobile. The layout works, but text was tricky to read, which we did change for a more readable color later. I also tweaked the filter so there was only a few pills and moved the events to the top of the events boxes.


Figure C - Participants click on the “Learn more” button.

Users will be able to not only see more information of events they are interested in, but they will also have the option to add it to their calendars so they don't miss out.

Figure A - Lo-fi Mentors Page

I also needed to showcase our mentors leading the events.


Figure B - Hi-fi Mentors Page

I made sure to include their LinkedIn for networking.


Success Metrics

235 Participants, 64% first time builders 28 projects submitted 25 technical and soft skill workshops

Conclusion

This was my very first project where I collaborated with a cross-functional team and have a live website. I learned a lot about communication consistency, receiving and implementing feedback from my team, and working with developers to create a fully functioning website. I also learned the important of having a stronger designer voice and having more confidence in my mockups and I can't wait to create more websites. With the very first virtual Buildathon, we had a great turnout. I am proud to be able to set up a foundation for the next following Buildathons and I can't wait to see what the future holds.
