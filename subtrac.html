Subtrac

Track your subscriptions instantly!

Project Brief

Subtract, a budget and subscription tracker that will give you a “big-picture” view of your expenses and warn you of any upcoming payments. Email searches of subscriptions make it easy for users to find all their subscriptions and prevent any forgotten accounts.

Project Role Timeline Platform
UX Designer December 2023 - January 2023 Mobile application

Here we have the final frames of the homepage showing graphs of spending and an option to add subscriptions.

Project Goals Key Challenges Target Users
Assist users in tracking their subscriptions and visualize their spending How can I help users visualize their spending? Is there an easier way to find subscriptions with minimal input? Young to middle aged adults who have multiple subscription services.

Introducing the Hero

Meet Adam, a 32 year old full-time professional, married to his wife Imani, for 7 years. They have two young children together. He has several subscriptions that he manages for him and his family including video streaming, meal delivery, software, and gym memberships. He would love a way to track all his subscriptions and keep track of his spending.

Understanding the problem

How can we make sure Adam doesn't forget all his subscriptions? Adding every single subscription to track. How do we visualize his spending to make it easier to understand?

Research and Discovery

A quick Google Survey that I sent out to a few friends to gain some insights on how I can design a helpful subscription tracking application.

Ideating & Brainstorming

  • Figure A: Adam wants to be able to see his budget and funds at a glance. So I created sketches of what kind of graphs should be included in the home screen.
  • Figure B: I got some great ideas such as using pie and line graphs, using the same email from sign-in, and a end-of-year wrap that was inspired by Spotify wrap.
  • Figure C: I thought of a renewal warning and an email search. There were also some fun ideas like a juggler game based on all the subscriptions that the user has added.

Prototyping

The homepage of Subtrac shows upcoming payments and graphs. After researching, I decided to change the bell curve graph with bar graphs, since bell-curve graphs is helpful for data like testing scores. Also, bar graphs are much more simple to show Adam how much he have been spending.

I also made an easy way for Adam to add his subscriptions from just providing an email. All he would have to do is press the plus icon and log in. I got this idea from using Mint for my own personal use.

Conclusion

By just working on this by myself, I was able to get some very helpful user feedback and ideate based on a simple Google survey. If given a chance, I would love to be able to add the features of Subtrac to an established application with a dedicated team of experienced designers. I had a solid idea of how to help people like Adam, but would love to be able to seek feedback from designers and test in different stages.
